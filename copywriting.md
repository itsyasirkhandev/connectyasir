# Copywriting System Guide

> **Stack:** Modern SaaS · Conversational · Performance-Focused · March 2026

This document is the authority for all copy and content across this project. Its purpose is to prevent generic, "corporate-sounding" or "AI-generated" text. Follow it strictly to produce world-class, human-first copy.

---

## The Prime Directive: Don't Sound Like a Corporation (or AI)

Most marketing copy is immediately recognizable — it's safe, passive, and filled with buzzwords. Avoid:

- **"Innovative solutions" or "Next-generation platforms."** Vague claims mean nothing.
- **Passive voice.** It sounds like a legal disclaimer. "Your data is managed by us" vs "We manage your data."
- **Speaking in the 3rd person.** "Acme Corp believes..." is corporate distance. Use "We" and "You."
- **Generic "supercharge your productivity" headlines.** These are the cargo shorts of copywriting.
- **Jargon-heavy descriptions.** If an 8th grader doesn't understand it, rewrite it.

Copy should feel like a **warm, direct conversation** with an expert who cares about your problem.

## The "Apple" Standard: Minimalist Precision

Distill complex technical capabilities into short, punchy, aspirational phrases.

- **Conciseness is quality.** Smaller word counts usually mean higher impact.
- **Benefit-first.** Never lead with a technical feature. Lead with the _feeling_ or _outcome_ of using it.
- **Power words.** Use visceral, high-impact verbs and adjectives: _Impressive, Supercharged, Effortless, Beautiful._

| Level        | Role      | Style                | Example                                         |
| :----------- | :-------- | :------------------- | :---------------------------------------------- |
| **Headline** | Attention | Benefit + Clarity    | "Run all day without blisters."                 |
| **Subhead**  | Interest  | Result + Specificity | "Ship your orders in 1 day, guaranteed."        |
| **Body**     | Desire    | Conversational Proof | "We built the tool we wanted to use ourselves." |
| **CTA**      | Action    | Low-friction         | "Get started in 60 seconds."                    |

## Psychology of Persuasion (Gestalt Laws of Copy)

Like design, great copy follows laws of human psychology.

### 1. The Rule of One

Every page, every email, every ad must have **One** target audience, **One** big idea, and **One** single call to action.

- ❌ Don't ask a user to "Sign Up" AND "Follow on Twitter" in the same focal point.

### 2. The 80/20 Rule

Spend **80%** of your effort on the headlines and **20%** on the body copy.

- If the headline fails, the rest of the page never gets read.
- **Rule:** A headline must be "Magnetic" — it should pull the reader into the next sentence.

### 3. Law of Specificity

Vague statements are forgettable. Specificity is convincing and builds trust.

- ❌ "Our tools make you faster."
- ✅ "Save 2.5 hours every Monday with automated reporting."

### 4. Law of Similarity (Customer Voice)

People trust people who sound like them. Use the **exact phrases and patterns** your customers use in interviews.

- **Action:** Talk to customers. Record their words. Use them verbatim in your copy.

### 5. Eyeball Monkey Bars

Structure your copy so the reader's eye "swings" from one point to the next (e.g., Short headline -> Bolded key phrase -> Bullet point -> CTA).

- **Rule:** Never use "Walls of Text." Every paragraph must be 3 lines or fewer.

### 6. Test & Humanize (The Perkins Playbook)

Never assume your first draft is the best.

1. **Forecast:** Choose a formula (PAS, AIDA).
2. **Draft:** Write it in a human, conversational voice.
3. **Refine:** Remove 20% of the words.
4. **Test:** A/B test headlines and CTAs to see what actually converts.

## Copy Clichés — Never Default To These

These are AI's comfort zone. If you're about to use one, stop and reconsider:

| Cliché                        | Why It Sounds "AI"  | Better Alternative                        |
| :---------------------------- | :------------------ | :---------------------------------------- |
| "Unlock your potential"       | Meaningless fluff   | "Build your first app tonight."           |
| "Leading provider of..."      | Corporate arrogance | "We help 500+ teams ship faster."         |
| "Seamless integration"        | Technical jargon    | "Works with the tools you already use."   |
| "Revolutionize your workflow" | Over-indexed hype   | "Stop wasting time on manual data entry." |
| "Best-in-class performance"   | Unverifiable claim  | "Loads in under 100ms."                   |
| "A different way of thinking" | Passive / Clunky    | "Think different."                        |

## Proven Copywriting Formulas

Use these templates to structure your narrative. Never "guess" a layout.

### PAS (Problem — Agitation — Solution)

_The most effective formula for landing pages._

1. **Problem:** Identify a specific pain point (e.g., "Your website is slow.")
2. **Agitation:** Make the pain visceral (e.g., "Every second of lag costs you 10% in sales.")
3. **Solution:** Present your product as the only cure (e.g., "We make your site load in 50ms.")

### AIDA (Attention — Interest — Desire — Action)

_The classic for sales pages and ads._

1. **Attention:** Hook them with a benefit headlines.
2. **Interest:** Flesh out why it matters.
3. **Desire:** Show them life with the product (the "After" state).
4. **Action:** Tell them exactly what to do next.

### BAB (Before — After — Bridge)

_Best for short social posts or case studies._

1. **Before:** The current, painful state.
2. **After:** The idealized, successful state.
3. **Bridge:** Your product is how they get from Before to After.

## Writing Mechanics (Non-Negotiable)

- **Write to One Person.** Find a picture of a single target customer. Write as if you are speaking directly to them.
- **Use the word "You."** It makes the reader the hero of the story. Turn "People want X" into "You want X."
- **Active Voice Only.** It's more direct and uses fewer words. "France beat Brazil" is better than "Brazil was beaten by France."
- **Grammatical Consistency.** All items in a list must follow the same pattern (e.g., all start with an active verb).
- **Avoid 3rd Person.** Avoid "Acme Company believes..." Use "We believe..." or "I believe..."
- **Fight Jargon.** Replace "Supercharge your productivity" with "Complete your to-do list in half the time."

## Copy Review Checklist

Before shipping any copy:

**The Human Test**

- [ ] Does this sound like a real person talking?
- [ ] Could I say this out loud to a friend without feeling awkward?
- [ ] Does it use "You" more than "We" or "I"?
- [ ] Is it written to **one** specific person?

**Clarity & Precision**

- [ ] Is every sentence in the active voice?
- [ ] Are there any vague claims (e.g., "best," "fastest") without proof?
- [ ] Are there at least 3 specific numbers or outcomes in the copy?
- [ ] Was the "Cliché Table" checked for banned phrases?

**Structure**

- [ ] Did I spend 80% of my time on the headlines?
- [ ] Do lists follow a consistent grammatical pattern?
- [ ] Is there a clear focal point (The "Rule of One")?
- [ ] Are the "Eyeball Monkey Bars" in place (no walls of text)?
- [ ] Is the reading level grade 4-6? (Use Hemingway Editor test).

**Persuasion**

- [ ] Does it follow a proven formula (PAS, AIDA, or BAB)?
- [ ] Does the CTA focus on the benefit, not the task (e.g., "Get My Guide" vs "Submit Form")?
- [ ] Is the primary benefit visible within the first 3 seconds of reading?
